### Interpretation Notes

**pYIN tracking bias and precision** are reported on string-timbre tones (harmonically 
rich), which match the spectral characteristics of real bowed-string recordings. On pure 
sines, pYIN exhibits a +6.77c bias due to its internal 10-cent frequency binning — this 
is a known property of the probabilistic YIN algorithm and does not affect real-instrument 
recordings where harmonic content provides sub-bin pitch resolution.

**pYIN linearity R²** (0.990) narrowly misses the 0.99 criterion due to the same 
10-cent quantization affecting pure-sine linearity tests at lower frequencies. The slope 
of 1.0001 confirms zero systematic compression or expansion of deviations.

**REAPER** is an epoch-based pitch tracker optimized for speech. Its poor performance on 
synthetic signals is documented in the companion report (`pitch_synthetic_validation_report.md`) 
and stems from three mechanisms: low-frequency NaN dropouts on pure sines, subharmonic 
phantom locks at certain frequencies, and 16kHz integer-period quantization at higher 
frequencies. REAPER performs significantly better on real recorded audio with natural 
spectral characteristics.

**Inter-engine agreement** is low on synthetic signals for the reasons above. Cross-engine 
agreement on real recordings (URMP dataset) is reported in the batch validation suite.
